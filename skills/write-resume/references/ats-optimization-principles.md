# ATS Optimization Principles

This file distills the attached PDF `ATS-Friendly Resume Optimization Strategy.pdf` into practical rules for rewriting resumes.

## Core Idea

Treat the resume as both a human document and a machine-readable data asset. The goal is not keyword stuffing. The goal is structural clarity, semantic alignment with the job ad, and credible evidence.

## Structural Rules

- Use a single-column layout with a clean top-to-bottom reading order.
- Keep critical information in the main body, not in headers or footers.
- Avoid tables, text boxes, sidebars, icons, charts, progress bars, and decorative graphics.
- Prefer standard section titles:
  - `Summary`
  - `Skills`
  - `Professional Experience`
  - `Education`
  - `Certifications`
- Use plain bullets and consistent date formatting.
- Assume reverse-chronological structure unless the user or market explicitly requires otherwise.

## Keyword Rules

- Mirror the job description's exact terminology when it is truthful for the candidate.
- Preserve important versioned or exact terms such as `Python 3.x`, `AWS`, `Azure`, `Kubernetes`, `SOC 2`, or `FinOps`.
- When helpful, use both the full term and acronym:
  - `Search Engine Optimization (SEO)`
  - `Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA)`
- Place the highest-priority phrases in three places:
  - summary or headline
  - skills section
  - recent relevant experience bullets
- Do not dump keywords in a list without evidence. Tie them to outcomes, ownership, scope, or delivery context.

## Bullet Point Rules

- Prefer action-plus-result bullets over task-only bullets.
- Use STAR or XYZ-style framing:
  - what was done
  - how it was done
  - what changed because of it
- Include numbers whenever the source material supports them:
  - percentages
  - time saved
  - latency reduction
  - cost reduction
  - team size
  - revenue or budget impact
  - scale or transaction volume

## Title And Relevance Rules

- Use the exact target role title in the summary or headline when it is a fair description of the candidate's fit.
- Do not change historical job titles to match the ad.
- Pull the most relevant experience higher in the document through ordering, summary emphasis, and tighter bullets.

## Anti-AI-Smell Rules

- Avoid generic office-speak such as `dynamic`, `innovative`, `results-driven`, `passionate`, or `detail-oriented` unless the source already uses them naturally.
- Vary sentence structure so every bullet does not read the same way.
- Keep the tone direct and specific.
- Use AI as a gap-analysis and rewriting assistant, not as a fact generator.
- Never invent skills, certifications, industries, years of experience, team sizes, or metrics.

## File And Submission Rules

- Prefer `.docx` for online ATS portals when the user needs a final file recommendation.
- Prefer text-based PDF for direct recruiter email when layout preservation matters.
- If you suspect the document may become image-based or heavily designed, recommend a simpler text-first version.
- Use the plain-text test mentally or explicitly: the resume should still make sense if converted to raw text.

## Platform Notes

### Workday

- Favor a very standard chronological structure.
- Make the `Skills` section explicit and easy to parse.
- Keep dates, titles, employers, and locations consistent.

### Greenhouse

- Keep the resume, LinkedIn profile, and any cover letter aligned on titles, dates, and core claims.
- Clean modern formatting is usually fine, but consistency still matters.

### Lever

- Expect the resume to remain searchable in the platform after this application.
- Use durable keyword phrasing that would still make sense for future related roles.

## Regional Notes

### US, UK, Canada, and similar ATS-heavy markets

- Prefer a concise one- to two-page resume.
- Exclude photo, age, marital status, and other personal data unless the target market explicitly expects it.
- Keep the tone results-focused and professional.

### DACH and more formal European markets

- Use only if the user is targeting that market.
- The user may want a more formal tone and a different set of conventions.
- Do not assume the US-style document is universally correct.

## Recommended Tailoring Sequence

1. Extract the role title and top requirements from the job ad.
2. Rank the keywords by importance and frequency.
3. Map each keyword to proof from the existing resume.
4. Rewrite the summary and skills section first.
5. Rewrite the most relevant experience bullets next.
6. Remove design or phrasing choices that hurt ATS readability.
7. Do a final pass for truthfulness, natural voice, and coverage.
