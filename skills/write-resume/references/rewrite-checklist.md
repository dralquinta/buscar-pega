# Resume Rewrite Checklist

Use this checklist after producing a tailored resume draft.

## Minimum Deliverables

- a tailored summary or headline
- an updated skills section aligned to the job ad
- revised experience bullets for the most relevant roles
- a short note on uncovered gaps or unsupported requirements

## Content QA

- Does the resume clearly target the actual job title?
- Are the most important tools, platforms, and domain terms mirrored from the job ad where truthful?
- Are the strongest keywords present in the summary, skills, and recent experience?
- Are the most relevant achievements quantified when the source supports it?
- Are unsupported requirements excluded from the resume body?
- Are employment dates, titles, and employer names unchanged unless the source itself was wrong?

## ATS QA

- Single-column structure only.
- Standard section headings only.
- No tables, text boxes, icons, or visual rating bars.
- No critical content in headers or footers.
- Plain bullets and consistent punctuation.
- Resume would still read correctly as plain text.

## Tone QA

- Language sounds like a real candidate, not a generic AI assistant.
- No repetitive openings across all bullets.
- No inflated adjectives or filler phrases.
- Tone matches the job ad and the target market.

## Recommended Response Pattern

When replying in chat, prefer this order:

1. `Targeting summary`
State the job title, the top themes pulled from the ad, and the positioning strategy.

2. `Tailored resume draft`
Provide the rewritten content in a clean, copy-ready format.

3. `Gap notes`
List missing or partially supported requirements that were not inserted into the resume.

## Safe Constraint Examples

Use constraints like these while rewriting:

- keep bullets concise and outcome-oriented
- preserve factual accuracy
- mirror exact terminology from the job ad when supported
- avoid buzzwords and generic filler
- keep the layout ATS-safe
